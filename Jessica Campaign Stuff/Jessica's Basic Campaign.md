# Campaign Introduction

Nestled in the rolling lowlands between the **Northreach Mountains** and the broad, glimmering **River Cail**, the frontier of **Aldenvire** marks the edge of true civilization. Here, the high mountain peaks fade into soft hills blanketed with heather and dotted by grazing sheep. A long, cobbled road winds from the capital far to the south, threading through misty passes, narrow bridges, and sweeping green valleys where travelers still swap stories of trolls, dragons, and old kings. The air carries the smell of rain-soaked grass and chimney smoke, and the sky, though often gray, feels alive with the restless energy of the approaching winter.

To the west stretches are the **Tanglewood**, a sprawling forest where the canopy deepens from birch to oak, concealing game trails, hunting lodges, and the occasional set of moss-covered standing stones whose runes hum faintly on moonlit nights. Children whisper of lights that drift between the trees, and hunters tell stories—half in jest—of shapes moving where no wind stirs. To the east, fertile farmland rolls toward the River Cail, whose steady current feeds the fields and mills before spilling into **Lake Merrin**, a mirror of sky and mist.

Along the river’s bend lies the town of **Brookhollow**, a sturdy but cheerful market town known for its fine cheeses, wool, and carved riverstone trinkets. The air often carries the scent of curd and oakwood smoke from the **Brookhollow Creamery**, where wheels of pale gold cheese age on open racks beneath timber lofts. South of town, the slopes near Lake Merrin host tidy rows of **Harrowvine grapes**, pressed in cool cellars beneath the vintner’s hall and shipped in casks stamped with Brookhollow’s rivercrest seal. From the nearby hills, miners haul small but precious finds—shards of sapphire and streaks of tourmaline—traded to the kingdom’s jewelers or to **Master Corven**, the town’s resident mage, who binds faint enchantments into the cut stones using runes taken from the standing stones in the Tanglewood.

Every third week hails the town's marketday, and the square fills with the noise of wagons and the cry of merchants. River barges moor at the wooden docks, loading crates of wine, wool, and enchanted gems in exchange for tools, salt, and fabrics from the southern cities. The **Silver Road** that winds through town ends here, the final outpost before the treacherous **Frostspire Pass** far north in the mountains, where jagged cliffs mark the border with the rival kingdom of Durnhal. Beyond that, few dare to travel. Brookhollow stands as Aldenvire’s farthest reach—a place of honest work, warm hearths, and the quiet hum of distant adventure.

# Location Introductions

**The Silver Road (Approaching Brookhollow)**  
The cobbles of the **Silver Road** glisten faintly with the morning’s rain, winding like a gray ribbon through hills of wet grass and heather. On one side, the River Cail murmurs against its stony banks; on the other, pastureland rises in soft folds dotted with sheep and low stone fences. The air is cool and clean, carrying the scent of wet earth and woodsmoke drifting from unseen chimneys ahead. Around the next bend, a handful of rooftops come into view—thatch and timber shining under a pale sun. Wagons creak past, their drivers waving in easy greeting, and a wooden sign sways gently in the breeze: **Brookhollow — Trade, Rest, and Good Company**. Beyond it, the town waits, lively yet small, the last touch of civilization before the wild northern passes.

**Brookhollow Town Square**  
The **Brookhollow Square** bursts with color and sound. Stalls crowd around the central well, their canvas awnings dripping faintly from the morning dew. Merchants call out their prices over the clatter of hooves and chatter of townsfolk. The air smells of baked bread, sweet grapes, and oiled leather. A bard strums near the fountain, his tune carried by the wind as children chase one another between barrels of cheese and baskets of apples. The wooden facades lean close together here, warm light spilling from every open doorway. Above it all, the creak of a hanging sign marks the **Riversong Tavern**, its windows fogged with laughter and ale.

**The Brookhollow Creamery**  
Down by the river’s bend, the **Brookhollow Creamery** hums with quiet industry. White smoke curls from the low chimney, carrying the scent of milk, brine, and oakwood ash. Inside, racks of cheese wheels line cool stone walls, each one marked with a carved seal of the town’s crest. Farmers unload pails of fresh milk from their wagons, chatting easily with the cheesemaker’s apprentices who stir great copper kettles by the hearth. The building itself is a sturdy timber hall, worn but proud, with broad shutters thrown open to the misty air. Outside, a pair of cats lounge among milk crates, and the sound of the river mingles with the lowing of cows across the fields.

**Master Corven’s Tower**  
Just beyond Brookhollow’s palisade stands **Master Corven’s Tower**, rising crookedly above a patch of overgrown garden. The tower’s stones are veined with moss and rune carvings, faintly aglow when dusk settles. A wooden door, reinforced by iron bands, bears a curious symbol—part arcane seal, part warning. Strange lights flicker in the upper window, and the faint scent of ozone mingles with herbs drying on lines outside. Corven himself is rarely seen except when fetching supplies or lecturing those who wander too close. Yet locals say his magic keeps the worst of the Tanglewood’s spirits at bay. The tower looms as both a curiosity and a quiet comfort to Brookhollow’s people—proof that a little magic still lives at the edge of the kingdom.

**The Tanglewood**  
West of Brookhollow, the land gives way to the wild green of the **Tanglewood**, where the air grows still and the light turns dappled. Birch and oak trees twist together in dense knots, their roots gripping mossy stone and their leaves whispering secrets with every breeze. The forest floor is soft with fallen needles and mushrooms, alive with birdsong and the occasional distant snap of a branch. Old hunters tell of rune-marked stones hidden deep within, said to hum under moonlight and glow faintly when storms approach. To some, the Tanglewood is a place of wonder—rich with herbs and game. To others, it is best left to the owls and the unseen eyes that watch from the shade.

**Harrowvine Slopes**  
South of town, the **Harrowvine Slopes** rise in neat, terraced hills above Lake Merrin, their rows of vines heavy with late-summer grapes. The scent of crushed fruit drifts through the valley, mingling with damp soil and the faint tang of oak casks. Workers hum old songs as they prune and gather, and a lone bell rings from the vintner’s hall each afternoon to mark the end of labor. At sunset, the hills glow gold and violet, and the reflection of the vines shimmers on the lake below. Barrels bound for the docks roll down the dirt road toward Brookhollow, carrying the valley’s laughter and its sweet, sharp wine.

**Lake Merrin**  
Beyond the vineyards lies **Lake Merrin**, calm as glass and edged with pale reeds that whisper when the wind passes through. Fishermen’s boats dot its surface, their reflections long and thin in the gray-blue water. The lake stretches wide enough to catch the morning fog, and in the chill of dawn, the world feels hushed and endless. On its northern shore, families picnic by the shallows while herons stand motionless in the distance. When the clouds break, the sun scatters silver light across the ripples—a sight said to bring good luck to anyone starting a journey.

**The Docks of Brookhollow**  
Down where the River Cail bends slow and deep, the **Brookhollow Docks** bustle from dawn till dusk. Wooden piers jut into the current, slick with rain and busy with ferrymen shouting over the creak of ropes. Barges heavy with wine barrels, wool bales, and cheese crates wait their turn at the water’s edge, while smaller fishing boats bob idly nearby. The air smells of wet timber, tar, and river mud, with a hint of grapes and smoke from the town beyond. A carved post at the dock’s end bears the town’s crest and a weathered inscription: _“Trade binds us stronger than stone.”_ It is here that Brookhollow greets the world—and sends it on its way again.

**The Riversong Tavern**  
In the heart of town stands the **Riversong Tavern**, the beating heart of Brookhollow’s laughter and rumor. Its timber beams are low and dark from years of hearth smoke, and its windows glow warmly against the drizzle outside. Inside, the scent of roasted lamb and sweet cider fills the air, and a fiddler plays from a corner near the fire. Traders rub elbows with farmers, and newcomers find themselves welcomed with a nod and a tankard. A large map of Aldenvire hangs behind the bar, peppered with pins and notes left by passing travelers. Above the mantle, carved into the wood, reads the tavern’s motto: _“Stories flow like rivers—drink deep.”_

**Frostspire Pass**  
Far beyond Brookhollow, where the road narrows and the air grows thin, lies the **Frostspire Pass**. Sheer cliffs rise on either side, their peaks lost to drifting snow. The wind here howls like a living thing, carrying echoes that sound almost like voices. Stone cairns mark the path, left by those who crossed safely—or not at all. Few dare the journey, for beyond the pass lies the rival kingdom of Durnhal, its watchtowers faintly visible through the fog on clear days. From Brookhollow, the pass is a rumor and a warning, a distant line where stories end and danger begins.

**The Glimmerwell Emporium**  
Tucked between a weaver’s loft and the cooper’s yard stands **The Glimmerwell Emporium**, Brookhollow’s most curious shop. Its crooked sign—painted with a swirl of blue and gold—creaks softly in the wind, and faint light seeps through the window shutters even at midnight. Inside, shelves overflow with oddities: crystal phials that shimmer like trapped moonlight, wands bound in ivy, and runestones etched with flickering sigils. The air hums faintly with enchantment, smelling of parchment, dust, and lavender oil. The shop’s proprietor, **Elda Glimmerwell**, is a half-elf with ink-stained fingers and a calm, knowing smile. She claims to source her wares from “honest trade and the occasional miracle,” though no one can quite say how she acquires them all.

Locals come for charms of good harvest and minor healing draughts, while travelers seek rarer wonders—an ever-burning lantern, a self-mending cloak, or stones said to whisper in one’s sleep. Elda keeps her finest stock in a locked cabinet behind the counter, where faint blue light leaks through the gaps. She speaks softly to her customers, asking as often about their dreams as their coin, and more than one adventurer has left her shop with both an item and an unanswered question.

## Act I — The Riversong Beginning (Roleplay, Setup, Shopping)

**Scene:** The _Riversong Tavern_, early evening.  
Rain streaks the windows as traders crowd near the fire, swapping stories about a _merchant barge that never arrived from the capital_. The docks below town are half-empty, and tension rides the air.
- **Hook:** A town guard, **Sergeant Brannor**, posts a notice seeking volunteers to investigate the wreckage upriver.
- **Personal Tie:** Rumors say _a sea-elf man_—George—was last seen helping load that same barge before it departed.
- **Purpose:** The party volunteers, partly for coin, partly for answers.
- **Optional Flavor:** Shopping or roleplay stops at the **Glimmerwell Emporium** and local stalls before departure.

# Character Backstory

Jessica's characters are:
- Aurora, Sea-Elf, Fighter
- Belle, Fairy, Wizard

**Aurora, Sea-Elf, Marine, Fighter**  
Born to a family of proud adventurers from the capital, **Aurora** spent her youth learning the ways of sword and sail. Her father, a renowned sea-elf explorer, perished years ago on an ill-fated expedition, leaving both his estate and his legacy in her hands. Recently, Aurora uncovered mention of her estranged uncle, **George**, who had long ago set out to seek a quieter life somewhere near the frontier town of Brookhollow. With little more than rumor to guide her, Aurora now journeys northward with her lifelong friend Belle, determined to uncover her uncle’s fate—and perhaps reclaim a piece of her family’s story.

**Belle, Fairy, Sage, Wizard**  
A bright and curious fairy raised among books and battle maps, **Belle** grew up in the company of adventurers. Her parents once fought beside Aurora’s father and often told tales of his daring, binding their families in friendship. When Aurora chose to seek her lost uncle, Belle joined her without hesitation, eager to lend her knowledge and magic to the cause. Her parents, now happily retired, promised to tend the sea-elf estate in the capital until the pair’s return. For Belle, this journey is as much about discovery as it is about loyalty—a chance to see the world she has only read about and stand beside the friend she’s known all her life.

*George, Sea-Elf*  
**George** is the younger brother of Aurora’s late father, a sea-elf who once shared the family’s restless spirit. Years ago, weary of endless quests and close calls, he penned a final letter announcing his intent to leave adventuring behind. His destination: the distant frontier, near a small riverside town called Brookhollow. No word has come from him since, leaving only whispers and fading ink to mark his trail.
